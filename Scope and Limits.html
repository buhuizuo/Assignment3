Scope and Limits

     Our application is mainly aimed at elderly users and patients with chronic diseases, so as mentioned above, our application should be very simple and direct, so that non professionals can understand the main functions and use of the application easily.

Our design is to work either as a standard app, or in conjunction with a user's physical Webster-Pak. The app may possibly function as a Chatbot, a traditional app, or perhaps a combination of both. It would allow users to input medications, dosage amounts, schedule of when to take medication, reminders, and logs.

At present, the main application modules we can achieve are divided into two parts: User input drug use data and Reminder. In order to make it easier for users to input correct drug information, we have conceived many data input methods, including the most direct text input of drug name, dosage, taking time and other details. The second method is voice interactive input: the voice prompts the user to read out the required information and comfirm, which is very friendly to the elderly who can't see clearly. The third is the scanning function, which scans the Medical Records issued by doctors or pharmacist, automatically searches for needed information, and automatically generates plans and implements them. The last one is to cooperate with doctors or pharmacists, resume user accounts, send the drug information needed by patients to the designated account mailbox in the form of e-mail, read the contents of the e-mail, and automatically generates medication plans and implements them.
The second modules, to remind the user to take medicine is a relatively simple one, vibration, flicker and alarm. If the user does not click the bottom to tell the application that they ºve taken medicines, he will be reminded again in a few minutes.

Users can also view or change their medication schedule. In the future, we will further extend the other functions we envision.
